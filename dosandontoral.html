Do's

1. Establish a consistent oral hygiene routine

2. Brush after breakfast/coffee in the am

3. Scape your tongue

4. Floss using proper techniqu

5. Use mouthwash to remove any remove any remaining debris

6. Drink plenty of water to maintain a neutral ph in the mouth

7. Midday advice use a xylitol based gum or mints to keep bacteria at bay

8. Use an automatic toothbrush

9. Use soft bristles

10. Rinse with water after drinking acidic beverages

Don't

1. Stay away from consuming excessive amounts of acidic beverages

2. Use alcohol based mouthwashes (dehydrates saliva flow, causes irritation and ulcers to tissues)

3. Consume large amounts of sugary or acidic based food/drinks

4. Stay away from toothbrush bristles that can be damaging to enamel and gum tissue

5. Be too aggressive with brushing (technique is more important than forced pressure)

6. Forget to brush before bed (water only after brushing before bed)

7. Sleep with mouth open (dries out mouth, leading to no saliva flow, which leads to increased risk for cavities and periodontal problems)

8. Overdo whitening toothpastes or bleaching treatments (can lead to sensitivity and thinning of enamel)
